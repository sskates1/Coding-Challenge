About



Exploring a tower at the Great Wall of China in the Juyongguan Pass


I am a Database Manager at Massachusetts General Hospital working in the Neurological Clinical Research Institute. My primary work is with the Northeast Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) Consortium managing and improving data collection in ALS clinical trials. I create and automate SQL logic checks to ensure data quality and consistency to allow for statistical analysis. I also develop and design Electronic Case Report Forms (eCRFs) and train clinical research coordinators and site investigators on the use of the Electronic Data Capture system.

I graduated from Georgia Institute of Technology in 2012 with a B.S. in Industrial and Systems Engineering. I studied simulation, optimization, supply chain modeling, stochastic manufacturing, regression, and forecasting. While at Georgia Tech I wrote a program that improved scheduling for operating rooms for a local hospital as part of a senior project.

In my spare time I enjoy surfing, rugby, and figure skating. I also enjoy board games and video games.
